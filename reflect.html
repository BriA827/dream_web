 In attempting to work in the computer science field, there are a number of financial issues I must consider. To be taken seriously in that field, a degree is a must. However, college is not cheap. According to EducationData.org, the average U.S. tuition is $27,279 for one year, or $109,116 for four years. Those numbers only include tuition. For all materials and living expenses, the yearly price increases to $35,551. I am not the child of a billionaire, unfortunately, and do not have that much money at my disposal. Unless my absent father is secretly rich and dies soon and leaves me money (which is only a fantasy I can wish for), I will have to procure the funds myself. As of last year, I was the tenth in my class of 200-something (and this year the gym midterm bumped me down to eleventh). I hope that by continuing to keep my grades and rank up, along with taking Honors and APs, some colleges will take pity on me and grant me scholarships. I dream of a full ride, as otherwise I most likely will not be able to afford college, but I know that’s only wishful thinking. I am just extremely hesitant to take out loans, as I have seen what happens when they consume your life, but, unfortunately, I will probably have to. Loans will add a lot of stress to my life, as I’ll then have to have a job while attending school, meaning I’ll be juggling everything and getting even less sleep than I do now. I do want to pursue higher education, but not at the risk of my financial security for the rest of my life. I have thought of enrolling in smaller colleges, but there lies the issue of how good the computer science programs will be.

 While doing this project, I’ve learned that the future seems even more daunting than before and I wish I never started planning. I’d prefer to just let stuff happen, and save myself the stress and worry. Going into computer science is something I want to do, but it’s not my dream. If things don’t work out, there’s other stuff I want to do in life too (graphic design, voice acting, stage acting, costume making, etc.).
